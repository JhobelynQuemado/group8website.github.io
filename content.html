What is Streaming?

A multimedia file that can be played instantly without having to be downloaded first is referred to as streaming. If a piece of media, whether recorded or live, can be viewed online and played on any device without being downloaded, it is considered a streamed content. It works through a continuous transmission of the media content from a server to the client. Prior to beginning our journey in streaming, we decide first on our content type for our stream and we choose our topic. In order to generate ideas and conduct preliminary research on the various streaming platforms, we employed the Design Thinking Process. By employing the Design Thinking Process, this will help and make streaming more familiar to us and help us learn more about it.

The following are the most common forms of streamed content:

These are only a few examples of the various types and forms of streaming content. During our EGs 3-4, we chose three content types, those are Podcasts, Informative Videos, and Educational Videos. We then chose Podcasts among the three of them during our EGs 5-6.

Viewers Analysis for Each Streaming Category

We conducted a survey for 32 students in our EG 3-4, and the result showed that 46.9% of respondents preferred Informative Videos, 34.4% preferred Podcasts,
and 18.8% preferred Educational Videos. Although the majority of the respondents preferred Informative Videos as our content type, we have decided to stick with Podcasts
as our content type. Why so? We chose Podcasts as our content type because Podcasts are more adaptable than trying to watch a video while doing something.
Podcasts have a far better technique of reaching our audience, which is why we chose them as our content type. Due to the ability to listen at one's own pace,
Podcasts encourage greater engagement. Perhaps one of the greatest pedagogic characteristics offered by educational podcasting is the chance to learn through listening.
To many of the current student generation, learning through listening is enjoyable and less tedious than reading.
